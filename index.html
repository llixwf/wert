Starfish

Starfish, or sea stars, are marine invertebrates belonging to the class Asteroidea. They typically have five arms, though some species have more, and a central disc. Their sizes range from a few centimeters to over 1 meter in diameter. Starfish use tube feet for movement and have a unique water vascular system. They lack a brain and blood, instead circulating seawater through their bodies for respiration and movement. Starfish can regenerate lost arms, a crucial survival trait. They are predators and scavengers, mainly feeding on mollusks like clams and oysters.
